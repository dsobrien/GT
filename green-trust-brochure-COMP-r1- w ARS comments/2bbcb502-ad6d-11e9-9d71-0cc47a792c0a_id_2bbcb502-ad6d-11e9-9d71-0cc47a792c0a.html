Traditional insurance companies are still too afraid to enter into the legalized cannabis industry for a variety

growing industry. Without the traditional lines of indemnification, the cannabis industry must once again

How does this work?
Tere are several steps to creating and operating a captive for the cannabis industry.
1
Banking
Because of Green Trust’s HIGH LEVEL OF VETTING both our client’s and the source of the moneys
used, we have banking depositories and processors that allow for our clients to have their cash
proceeds picked up by professional armored car transports, and delivered directly to the federal
reserve bank. Te money is then attributed to a Green Trust custodial account where it is held
and used to create your captive. All deposits are reported to all applicable federal institutions to
ensure that each and every dollar that is deposited into our pool is fully reported under the law.
2 Build Your Captive
Trough Green Trust’s team of attorneys, accountants, financial advisors, insurance underwriters,
actuaries, and specialists, we create, register, and capitalize the captive. Te captive is WHOLLY
OWNED BY YOUR ENTITY OR GROUP, and is managed by Green Trust.
3 Underwrite the Entity or Group
Once the captive is created and registered, policies are created and individual entity risk is assessed
for your entity or entities within a group. Tis creates the amount of premium that must be paid,
and informs you about the TAX WRITE-OFFS that are created.
4 Premium Transfer
Premiums are then collected again from your entity or group and deposited directly into the
Federal Reserve Bank in a Green Trust custodial account. Tese premiums are fully funded and
protect your entity or group for the policy year, just like any other insurance policy would - only
better because it COVERS EXACTLY WHAT YOU WANT it to cover.
5 Claims
If there are any claims made on any of your policies created, GREEN TRUST HANDLES THE
LEGITIMATE CLAIMS ADMINISTRATION, and uses its own law firm to vigorously defend against
frivolous claims and pay out any liability on behalf of the entity or group.
6 Return of Premiums
At the end of the policy period, the premium (minus management fees, legal fees, claims paid, and
cost of insurance) is RETURNED TO THE CAPTIVE. Unlike traditional insurers, you keep the the
money because it is “self-insurance”.
